Montie Wayne grew up in Goleta and never left. He started Montie Wayne Sheet Metal out of his garage, with a clear idea: do quality work, show up when you say you will, and understand the place you're working in. Three decades later, that's still the whole plan.

MWSM has grown into a full-service sheet metal fabrication and HVAC shop, with state-of-the-art bending and shear equipment, a crew of skilled craftsmen, and a client list that includes homeowners, general contractors, architects, and commercial property owners across Santa Barbara and the surrounding area.

We work on projects of all sizes — from a single custom chimney cap to multi-building commercial HVAC installations. What hasn't changed is who picks up the phone and who shows up on site. This is still a family business, and the people you talk to are the people who do the work.

Being local isn't just a tagline for us. It means we understand what Santa Barbara's architectural review board looks for, what the city inspectors expect, and what the coastal environment does to materials over time. It also means we care about this place the way a neighbor does — which shapes how we think about the materials we recommend and the systems we install. We've seen firsthand what the shift to heat pumps and high-efficiency systems means for homeowners here, and we help our clients make choices that hold up and make sense long-term.

Deep local roots

Why being local matters in Santa Barbara

Santa Barbara operates under some of the most stringent architectural and design review requirements in California. The city's design guidelines, Historic Landmarks Commission, and architectural review processes affect everything from material selection to paint colors — and sheet metal work is no exception.

Contractors who don't regularly work in this market often discover those requirements mid-project. The result is delays, change orders, and sometimes having to redo work that didn't meet city standards. We see it happen to out-of-area crews regularly.

After 30 years of pulling permits, working with city inspectors, and fabricating work that meets Santa Barbara's exacting standards, we know what's expected before the first measurement is taken. That knowledge saves our clients time, money, and headaches.
