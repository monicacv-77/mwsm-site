What is a metal roof?

Not just materials — a complete installed system.

A metal roof is different from the roofing metals we fabricate for other contractors. Those are components — drip edge, counter flashing, Z-bar — used in conventional roofing assemblies. A metal roof is the entire finished surface: panels, seaming, trim, and fastening systems, all fabricated in our shop and installed by our own crew.

The result is a roof that can last 50 years or more with minimal maintenance — far outlasting asphalt shingles — while offering superior fire resistance, better performance in coastal conditions, and a look that holds up to Santa Barbara's demanding design standards.

We fabricate everything in-house, which means tighter tolerances, faster turnaround, and full accountability from measurement to installation.

Roof systems

Four types. One shop. All installed by us.

01

Standing Seam

The modern standard for metal roofing — and the most common choice for high-end residential and commercial work in Santa Barbara. Concealed fasteners mean no exposed screws to leak or back out over time. Clean, architectural lines that work well with the city's design guidelines.

  • Concealed fastener system
  • Allows for thermal expansion
  • Available in a range of profiles and finishes
  • Excellent for low-slope applications

02

Corrugated Panel

A classic profile with wide application — residential, agricultural, and light commercial. More economical than standing seam, still durable and long-lasting. Works well on steeper pitches and in situations where a more relaxed aesthetic is appropriate.

  • Exposed fastener system
  • Cost-effective for larger areas
  • Multiple metal options including Galvalume and painted steel
  • Fast installation on straightforward roof forms

03

Architectural Panel

Custom profiles designed to meet specific project requirements — including those set by Santa Barbara's architectural review board. When a standard profile won't satisfy the design review, we fabricate something that will. This is where our in-house shop capability matters most.

  • Custom profiles fabricated to spec
  • Suited to design-reviewed projects
  • Works with historic and contemporary architecture
  • Full range of metals and finishes

04

Copper Roofing

For historically significant properties, high-end custom homes, and specialty applications where longevity and appearance are both paramount. Copper develops a natural patina over time that is well-suited to Santa Barbara's coastal environment — and often preferred by the city's historic review process.

  • 50–100+ year lifespan
  • Natural patina suited to coastal environments
  • Often required or preferred for historic properties
  • Fully recyclable at end of life

Why metal

The case for a metal roof in Santa Barbara

Santa Barbara's combination of coastal salt air, occasional high winds, fire risk, and strict design standards makes metal roofing a particularly strong choice here. These aren't just selling points — they're practical realities we've seen play out over 30 years of working in this environment.

Built to last 50+ years

A well-installed metal roof outlasts asphalt shingles by decades. In the long run, it's often the more economical choice when you factor in replacement costs.

Class A fire resistance

Metal is non-combustible and carries the highest fire rating available. In a region where wildfire risk is real, this matters — and may affect your insurance premiums.

Coastal durability

Salt air accelerates corrosion on many roofing materials. The right metal roof — Galvalume, painted steel, or copper — holds up far better in Santa Barbara's coastal environment.

Energy efficient

Reflective metal finishes reduce heat gain, lowering cooling loads in summer. Cool-roof rated products qualify for California energy code compliance and may reduce utility costs.

Sustainable choice

Most metal roofing contains significant recycled content and is 100% recyclable at end of life — unlike asphalt shingles, which are a major contributor to landfill waste.

Design flexibility

With the right profile and finish, metal roofing can satisfy Santa Barbara's most demanding architectural review requirements — from modern to Spanish Colonial.
